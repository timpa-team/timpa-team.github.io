Permaculture farm · Ackerperlen, Hamburg

Get Your Hands Dirty. Together.

A half-day on a working permaculture farm, for teams who want to do something that actually matters.

The experience

A team, a farm, and a few hours of real work: planting, harvesting, understanding where food actually comes from. Ackerperlen is a permaculture project outside Hamburg where environmental education happens through doing, not listening.

You will work alongside the people who run the farm. You will eat what it grows. And you will leave knowing something you did not know before.

How it works

  1. Arrive & get oriented

    A short tour of the farm and an introduction to permaculture: what it is, why it matters, and what your team will be working on today.

  2. Get into the soil

    A hands-on workshop on compost, living soil and ecological cycles. The kind of thing that sounds dry until you are holding a handful of earth and someone explains what is living in it.

  3. Work the farm

    Your team takes on a real section of the farm, and what you do depends on the season: planting in spring, harvesting in summer, composting in autumn. The work is real and the result stays on the farm long after you leave.

  4. Eat together

    A simple meal prepared from what the farm produces. On-site, unhurried, no agenda.

  5. Take something home

    A seedling, a small harvest, or just the knowledge of where your food comes from. Either way, you leave with more than you arrived with.

Why Ackerperlen

Ackerperlen runs on one belief: you only truly value what you understand, and you only understand what you have experienced yourself. The farm is a real working project, not a venue dressed up as one. Your booking directly supports their environmental education work with schools and communities across Hamburg.

Makerspace · WerkStadt Lüneburg

Build Something Real. Together.

A hands-on birdhouse workshop at WerkStadt Lüneburg, for teams who want more than a trust fall.

The experience

At WerkStadt Lüneburg, a community makerspace in the heart of the city, your team gets real wood, real tools, and a real challenge: build the most creative birdhouse you can. From scratch. In 90 minutes.

Four teams. Four very different birdhouses. And an evening around the fire to finish it off.

How it works

  1. Arrive & explore

    You get a tour of the WerkStadt and meet the people behind it, a community workspace built on the idea that making things together matters.

  2. Plan your build

    Each team sketches their design before touching a single piece of wood. What species are you building for? What is your concept? How wild can you go?

  3. Build it

    Saws, hammers, drills, paint. Experienced WerkStadt guides are on hand for safety and technique, but the creative decisions are entirely yours.

  4. Present & celebrate

    Every team gets two minutes to pitch their house. Then the group votes. Four categories, four winners, because great teamwork comes in different shapes.

Four winners, four categories
🏆 Most Creative Design
The one nobody saw coming
🐦 Most Bird-Friendly
Function meets feathers
🌱 Most Sustainable Build
Less waste, more thinking
😄 WerkStadt Spirit
The crowd favourite

  1. Wind down around the fire

    After the awards, the tools go down and the fire pit comes out. No agenda, no slides, just the team, the warmth, and whatever conversation happens naturally.

  2. Take it home

    Every birdhouse leaves with you. Put them up at your office, donate them, or scatter them across the city.

Why WerkStadt

WerkStadt Lüneburg is a nonprofit community workshop built on one idea: tools and machines should be used, not owned, and making things together builds more than just the object. Opened in 2024, it has become a creative hub for makers, tinkerers, and anyone who wants to work with their hands. Your booking directly supports their work and keeps the space alive for the community.

Inclusive sport · SIT'N'SKATE

Destroy a Few Stereotypes. Together.

An inclusive wheelchair-skate session with SIT'N'SKATE, where your team learns to ride, sees mobility from a completely new perspective, and helps break down a few prejudices along the way.

The experience

SIT'N'SKATE is an inclusion project with a mission they put right in their name: destroying stereotypes. They show that a wheelchair is not a dull grey aid but a cool, stylish piece of sports equipment, and they run wheelchair, skate and mobility courses that put participation and community first.

For this event, your team gets on wheels. You try wheelchair skating for yourselves, guided by experienced coaches, and experience firsthand what inclusion in sport actually feels like.

How it works

  1. Meet and warm up

    Get to know the SIT'N'SKATE team and the idea behind the project, then warm up and get comfortable on wheels.

  2. Learn the basics

    Coaches walk your team through the fundamentals of wheelchair skating: balance, control, and your first moves in a safe setting.

  3. Skate together

    Take on the ramps and the park as a team, cheer each other on, and try things you did not think were possible.

  4. Reflect and connect

    Sit down together afterwards to talk about what shifted: the perspective, the assumptions, and what inclusion can look like back at work.

Why SIT'N'SKATE

SIT'N'SKATE is a nonprofit project built to make society more inclusive and to challenge the prejudices people carry about disability and mobility. Your booking supports their inclusion work and their regular wheelchair-skate meet-ups, where participation and community come first.
